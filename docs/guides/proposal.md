# Business Proposal: Adopting Holocortex

*A framework for governed, cost-efficient, and resilient use of AI*

**Prepared for:** Business Leadership · **For internal review** · July 2026

---

## Executive summary

Most organisations are adopting AI faster than they are governing it. The
result is spend that no one can fully account for, the reasoning behind
important work disappearing when people move on, and a growing dependence on
individual AI vendors.

Holocortex is a lightweight framework and set of tools that makes AI use
measurable, governed, and portable. It caps and reports AI spend, records the
reasoning behind work so it survives staff turnover, applies enforceable rules
with an independent check before any AI-proposed action, and keeps the
organisation's methods independent of any single AI provider.

**This proposal recommends adopting Holocortex as standard practice for
AI-assisted work. Direct cost is low — it builds on tools already in use — and
the benefits compound as usage grows.**

## The business problem

Ungoverned AI adoption carries three costs that rarely appear on any single
budget line but accumulate quickly:

**1. Uncontrolled, invisible spend.** Premium AI is billed per use, and the
large majority of everyday usage is routine work that does not require the most
expensive engine. Without controls, that engine ends up handling commodity
tasks at premium rates, and the organisation has little visibility into how
much of the bill is avoidable.

**2. Knowledge that leaves with people.** The reasoning behind a decision — and,
just as valuable, the options that were considered and rejected — typically
lives in an individual's head or a disposable chat session. When that person
moves on, it is gone, and the same problems are solved again from scratch.

**3. Ungoverned risk and vendor lock-in.** AI can act confidently and be wrong,
with no independent review before its output is put to use. And dependence on a
single AI provider exposes the organisation to that vendor's pricing,
availability, and product decisions — none of which are within your control.

## The proposal

Adopt Holocortex as the standard way AI-assisted work is done. In business
terms it does four things:

- Routes work to the cheapest capable tool first and only escalates to premium
  AI when a task genuinely warrants it — under a spending cap, with every
  decision logged.
- Automatically records decisions and the reasoning behind them into a durable,
  searchable record the organisation owns.
- Enforces written rules on AI behaviour and runs an independent check of any
  AI proposal before it is acted upon.
- Keeps the organisation's knowledge and methods portable, so a change of AI
  vendor or model does not disrupt the work.

## Business benefits

| Benefit | What it means in practice | Business outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost control & visibility** | Routine work is handled by low-cost tools; premium paid AI is used only when a task needs it, under a spending cap you set. | Lower, predictable AI spend — and a report showing exactly where every pound went. |
| **Governance & auditable control** | Written rules the AI must follow, plus an independent automated check of any AI proposal before it is acted on. | A defensible answer to "how do you govern AI?" — with an audit trail, not a shrug. |
| **Knowledge continuity** | The reasoning behind decisions — including approaches tried and rejected — is captured automatically and kept. | Reduced key-person risk; problems are not re-solved from scratch when people move on. |
| **Vendor independence** | The organisation's methods and knowledge are held independently of any single AI provider. | Protection against a supplier's pricing, availability, or product changes. |
| **Automatic safeguards** | Mechanical checks catch mistakes — including confidential material heading somewhere it should not. | Prevented incidents rather than post-incident clean-ups. |

## Evidence

These are not hypothetical. During its own development the framework
demonstrated each benefit in practice:

- **Spend visibility:** in the framework's own development use, the low-cost
  tools handled 86% of all queries; the handful that escalated to premium AI
  each carried a logged reason, and the report shows every one.
- **Governance in action:** the independent check reliably caught unsafe or
  out-of-scope AI proposals before they were acted on, with a documented trail.
- **Safeguards that fired — and one that was learned:** pre-publication checks
  caught three inadvertent disclosures of internal names before anything left
  the building. One confidential document did slip through a gap in those
  checks; routine post-publication verification found it, it was withdrawn the
  same day, and the gap was closed mechanically so the same mistake is now
  blocked outright. Both halves are the point: prevention where possible, fast
  detection and honest correction where not.
- **Vendor independence, proven:** the underlying AI model was replaced partway
  through the work, and the work continued without a gap.

## Investment and effort

Direct cost is low but not zero. Holocortex is built on tools the organisation
is likely to have already, and it reduces premium-AI spend rather than adding
to it. The one hardware requirement is a machine capable of running the local
models — typically an existing GPU-equipped workstation or one modest dedicated
server; under-powered hardware is a false economy (development testing retired
one such host). Beyond that, the main investment is a modest amount of
discipline — following a light, repeatable way of working — and its value
compounds the more it is used. It is
infrastructure, not a one-off purchase: the return grows over time as the
record of decisions deepens and the cost controls take hold.

## Risks and considerations

In the interest of a balanced view:

- **It is not a silver bullet.** It reduces and governs cost and risk; it does
  not eliminate them, and it depends on people using it as intended.
- **Value is cumulative.** The largest benefits — institutional knowledge,
  spend trends, governance history — build over months, not on day one.
- **It requires light ongoing discipline.** The safeguards are largely
  automatic, but the practice of capturing decisions and reviewing AI output
  rewards consistency.

## Recommendation and next steps

Adopt Holocortex for AI-assisted work, beginning with a focused pilot so the
benefits can be measured directly:

1. Run a time-boxed pilot on one team or workflow that already uses AI.
2. Measure the before-and-after: premium-AI spend, and how much routine work is
   handled without it.
3. Review the governance and knowledge records the pilot produces.
4. On the evidence, expand it to standard practice across AI-assisted work.

**The proposition is simple: turn AI from an unmetered, ungoverned,
vendor-dependent cost into one that is capped, auditable, and portable — while
ensuring the reasoning behind your work outlives the people and the tools that
produced it.**
